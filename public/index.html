Red-Green Color Blindness

Red-Green color blindness is the most common form of color vision deficiency. It includes:

  • Protanopia: Lacking red cone cells.
  • Protanomaly: Reduced sensitivity to red light.
  • Deuteranopia: Lacking green cone cells.
  • Deuteranomaly: Reduced sensitivity to green light.

Blue-Yellow Color Blindness

Blue-Yellow color blindness is rarer and affects the ability to distinguish between blues and yellows:

  • Tritanopia: Lacking blue cone cells.
  • Tritanomaly: Reduced sensitivity to blue light.

Complete Color Blindness

Complete color blindness, or monochromacy, is when individuals see no color at all:

  • Rod Monochromacy: No functioning cone cells; vision is in shades of gray.
  • Cone Monochromacy: Only one type of cone cell works.
